QR Code Security Threats

Understanding the risks and how to protect yourself

Phishing QR Codes

Cybercriminals create QR codes that redirect to fake login pages designed to steal your credentials. These often mimic legitimate banking, social media, or email portals.

63% of phishing attacks use QR codes
3.2x more effective than email phishing

Protection Tips:

  • Always verify the URL before entering credentials
  • Look for HTTPS and legitimate domain names
  • Use bookmarks for important sites instead of scanning QR codes

Malware Distribution

Malicious QR codes can trigger automatic downloads of malware, ransomware, or spyware onto your device, often without your knowledge or consent.

45% increase in QR malware in 2024
82% target Android devices

Protection Tips:

  • Disable automatic downloads from QR codes
  • Keep your device's OS and apps updated
  • Use reputable security software on your devices

Payment Fraud

Fraudsters replace legitimate payment QR codes with their own, redirecting funds to criminal accounts instead of the intended recipient.

₹127M lost to QR payment fraud in 2024
74% target small businesses

Protection Tips:

  • Verify payment QR codes with the merchant before scanning
  • Use trusted payment apps with built-in security features
  • Double-check recipient details before confirming payments

How to Protect Yourself

Inspect Before Scanning

Check for signs of tampering or sticker overlays on QR codes in public places.

Use Secure QR Scanners

Choose scanners that preview URLs and check for known malicious sites before opening.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your device's OS and apps to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Enable Security Features

Use security features like two-factor authentication and app-specific passwords.
